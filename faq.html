FAQ

What does H.O.P.E. stand for? 
H.O.P.E. stands for the four things we strive to provide to cancer patients and their families: Help, Optimism, Peace, and Encouragement.
Is there an age restriction to volunteer?
H.O.P.E. Team members must be at least 18 years of age. If you are interested in volunteering and under the age of 18, check out our Teen Leadership Program.
What are the steps to apply to be a H.O.P.E. Team member?
  1. Complete online application
  2. Attend Volunteer Information/Orientation session (email invite will be sent when application is submitted)
  3. Complete paperwork and processes covered in the Information/Orientation session and complete a shadow session that will be set up individually.

Volunteer

The H.O.P.E. Team provides the volunteer program at Western Regional Medical Center. These volunteers a vital part of the team that provides hope, healing, and care to patients and families affected by cancer. If you are looking for a life-changing experience and are available 3-4 hours a week, then the H.O.P.E. Team volunteer team may be just what you are seeking.
