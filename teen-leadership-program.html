Teen Leadership Program

Teen Leadership Program Overview

  • The program is a six (6) week program during the Summer (Mid- June through the end of July) with the option to continue projects throughout the year. The program is geared towards involving 15-17 year olds in the operations of a hospital and leadership qualities required.
  • Signed parental permission is required for all participants.
  • Participants will apply through a process outlined in a separate document and selection of participants will be performed by the H.O.P.E. Team Board.
  • Interested persons must meet the following criteria to apply:
  1. Applicant must be at least 15 and no older than 17 years of age on the date they are applying.
  2. Applicant must currently have a GPA of 3.5 or higher (if home-schooled parental confirmation will suffice) for last completed quarter or semester.
  3. Applicant must provide an essay (at least 500 words and a maximum of 1000) on a topic given in the application process.
  4. Applicant must be available for a formal interview prior to placement.
  5. Applicant must be able and willing to attend ALL scheduled days during the six week commitment period. This will include 4 hour days 5 days per week.
  6. Applicant must provide negative TB testing results and complete a medical clearance document prior to placement.
  • This is an intense hands-on program and each participant will be assigned a mentor who will expect them to perform at a high level.
  • If participant is unable to meet requirements, they will be dismissed from the program without credit for hours participated to that point.
  • Program will be run through the H.O.P.E. Team Service Alliance, Inc. and participants will be considered volunteers while in the program. Routine volunteer screening rules and regulations will be in effect with the exception of background checks which shall be waived for minors.
  • Placement is specific departments will be at the sole discretion of the H.O.P.E. Team.
  • Applications may be obtained by visiting Cancer Treatment Centers of America from April 10th through May 10th. They are located at the Concierge/Security desk.
  • Teens approved to enter the final process will be notified by telephone and/or email and will be scheduled for an in-person interview.
  • Due to the high number of applicants expected, those not selected to continue the process will not be notified.
